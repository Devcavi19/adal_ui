1. PREFERRED AREAS OF SPECIALIZATION AMONG LEVEL 4 STUDENT NURSES OF CAMARINES SUR POLYTECHNIC COLLEGES, NABUA, CAMARINES SUR 
    Abstract: The researchers conducted this study to determine the preferred areas of specialization among Level 4 Student Nurses of Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges. At the same time, to identify the factors that influence the student nurses in considering a nursing specialization. The respondents of this study were 103 student nurses which was the total population of Level 4 pursuing Bachelor of Science in Nursing. The study sought answers regarding the profile of the respondents, their preferred area of specialization, the significant relationship between the respondents’ profile and preferred area of specialization, and the plan proposed to guide the Level 4 Student Nurses in their preferred area of specialization. The descriptive-correlational design was used in the study and the researchers utilized a questionnaire-checklist tool via Google forms. The statistical tools used to analyze the data were the percentage technique, weighted mean, and Chi-square test. The study revealed that the most preferred area of specialization among the respondents is Emergency Care Nursing & Critical Care Nursing, followed by Medical Surgical Nursing. The most common factors influencing the preferred area of specialization are personal interest and passion, working environment, knowledge and comprehension, & career development and future prospects. Moreover, the findings of the study show significant relationship between preferred area of specialization and profile in terms of sex assigned at birth but not in other biographic data are: Maternal Care Nursing, Pediatric Nursing, Emergency Care Nursing, Critical Care Nursing, and Rehabilitation Nursing. Meanwhile, the area of specialization that shows a significant relationship between preferred area of specialization and profile in terms of age but not in other biographic data is Institutional Nursing. On the other hand, there is no ignificant relationship between preferred area of specialization and respondents’ profile in areas of Medical-Surgical Nursing, Community & Public Health Nursing, Nephrology Nursing, and Occupational Nursing. Overall, the findings of this study may help to improve the level of understanding and competence of Level 4 Student Nurses. As well as, this may motivate, encourage and help to them to pursue ana area of specialization that suits their abilities, knowledge and skills.
 
2. KNOWLEDGE AND PRACTICES ON DENGUE FEVER AMONG THE RESIDENTS OF SELECTED BARANGAYS IN NABUA, CAMARINES SUR
    Abstract: Dengue Fever is a key contributor to various health challenges, particularly in regions where it's endemic. It can strain healthcare systems, cause significant morbidity, and in severe cases, lead to death. Despite understanding about the disease, this study likely investigates factors such as knowledge and practices of dengue fever among the selected barangays in Nabua, Camarines Sur. Additionally, the efforts to control mosquito populations and improve public health infrastructure are crucial in mitigating its impact. This study aimed to evaluate the knowledge and practices on dengue fever among the residents of selected barangays in Nabua, Camarines Sur. In terms of evaluating their knowledge about dengue fever—including its symptoms, transmission, and preventive measures—the study also evaluated their real actions on how to eliminate mosquito breeding grounds. To determine the extent of the knowledge of the residents and propose a plan that will enhance the knowledge of the respondents and strengthen their practices. The study utilized a survey method to gather data using questionnaires and disseminated among the 10 respondents of selected barangays of Nabua. The methodology of this study is the percentage technique, weighted mean, and the four-point Likert scale. The percentage technique was utilized as a statistical tool to accurately measure the responses, this approach made it easier to compare and evaluate the results and developments. Furthermore, the weighted mean was employed to calculate the respondents' ratings based on their knowledge and health practices regarding dengue. Additionally, the four-point likert scale allows the researchers to include options without a neutral choice. It is employed in the adaptive tool with the following interpretation for the respondents' chosen knowledge and practices. The results show that the majority of the respondents were 21 – 30 years old, female, single, elementary undergraduate, and college undergraduates. Their family's monthly income was less than Php 9,520. The respondents have a good understanding of dengue fever in terms of preventive measures, effects, and treatment, which is verbally interpreted as "knowledgeable". Followed by the management and mode of transmission which is verbally interpreted as "moderately knowledgeable". In addition, the respondents' employed practices of dengue fever in terms of search and destroy of breeding sites, and seeking early medical consultation were verbally interpreted as "practiced". Followed by the support fogging in areas with clustering cases, self- protection from mosquito bites, and sustain hydration which was verbally interpreted as "moderately practiced". Moreover, the proposed plan aims to enhance the knowledge of the respondents in the selected barangays in the municipality of Nabua, Camarines Sur, and strengthen their practices.

3. JOB SATISFACTION OF CLINICAL INSTRUCTORS AT CAMARINES SUR POLYTECHNIC COLLEGES, NABUA, CAMARINESSUR
    Abstract: This study aimed to comprehensively understand the job satisfaction of the clinical instructors at Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges, Nabua, Camarines Sur. The qualitative research design and phenomenological approach along with the data gathering tools specifically the semi-structured interview and voice recording was utilized to gather comprehensive data from the twenty-two (22) clinical instructors who served as the participants of the study. The gathered data were analyzed using the Creswell’s data analysis method. From the analysis of data, the results revealed that under the theme Working Field of the Clinical Instructors; Eleven participants verbalized that their job satisfaction is influenced by the Teamwork & Relationship with Colleagues and Students; Ten participants explained that their job satisfaction is influenced by their Passion & Commitment Towards Work; leven participants explained that Salary, Rewards, & Benefits influences their job satisfaction, but two of them verbalized that they are not financially satisfied enough; Four participants verbalized that the administration influences their job satisfaction. Meanwhile, under the theme Impacts of their Job to their Personal Lives; Four participants expressed their thoughts regarding the influence of having work life balance in their job satisfaction however, one participant expressed how moderately satisfied they were due to the fact that their schedule affects their quality time with their family; and also, Three participants shared that having professional and personal growth as part of their job, influences their job satisfaction. Furthermore, under the theme Benefit and Well-Being of the Students of CSPC; Ten participants shared that their job satisfaction is influenced by means of Imparting Knowledge and Skills as well as a Medium to the Student’s Success; however, one participant expressed that their job satisfaction is influenced by having a Comfortable Learning Environment which they also shared that they are not satisfied because of the inconveniences they experience while rendering the class discussions. The proposed plan of the study was formulated to improve and address the influencing factors of the job satisfaction of the clinical instructors at Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges, Nabua, Camarines Sur to help them achieve a good performance in their job as well as to improve their job satisfaction.

5. MENTAL HEALTH STATUS OF THE BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN NURSING STUDENTS OF CAMARINES SUR POLYTECHNIC COLLEGES 
    -This study aimed to assess the mental health status of the 3rd year Nursing Students of Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges. The study was based on a quantitative research design. The respondents of the study were one hundred fifty-five (155) randomly selected third (3rd) year nursing students in CSPC. Findings from the study showed that majority of the respondents were found to exhibit symptoms for moderate depression, extremely severe anxiety, and normal level of stress. In terms of coping strategies, results showed that the most common coping strategy employed by the respondents is distracting oneself by doing other diversional activities (watching movies/TV, listening to music, doing school tasks, etc.). Other common strategies employed by the respondents are positive reframing, humor, and active coping. Meanwhile, the lowest utilized coping strategy is venting to others, seeking emotional social support, and substance use. Moreover, among the profile indicators of age, sex, birth order, civil status, employment status of parents, and family monthly income, none of which were found to have a significant correlation with the respondents’ mental health status. On the other hand, there was a significant correlation between the mental health status of the respondents and the coping strategies they employ. The proposed plan of the study was formulated towards enhancing the coping strategies employed by the nursing students of CSPC. It includes the promotion of various activities, strategies and programs that can be implemented that are beneficial to the students in order to have better mental health outcomes and be able to cope with mental health challenges effectively. 

6. SAVING LIVES: FIRSTHAND EXPERIENCES OF STUDENT NURSES OF CAMARINES SUR POLYTECHNIC COLLEGES
    -Student Nurses have been deployed in and outside the school premises to render immediate first aid response in unprecedented circumstances. The aim of this study is to establish a qualitative description of the lived experiences of nursing students from CSPC in rendering first aid service and to identify plans in establishing an appropriate response and intervention as a prerequisite to enhance the student nurses’ lived experiences. Semi structured interviews were conducted with 10 nursing students who had participated in rendering first aid services. The interview data were transcribed and Creswell’s method was used to analyze the data and generate thematic analysis. The experiences of nursing students can be summarized into five themes: psychological quality, gap in emergency resources, need for professional competence, factors influencing first aider’s service, and satisfaction with self- improvement.  Findings from the study showed that nursing students should be able to manage uncertainty as this influence how they respond to certain circumstances. Meanwhile, due to the lack of resources, student nurses’ flexibility, creativity, and critical thinking skills in utilizing available resources improved, however, immediate response is delayed and affected. Moreover, professional competencies are seen to be vital for the student nurses to get used with unforeseen circumstances and heightened mind condition in regards to appropriate assessment and management. At the same time, nursing students should be characterized with higher self-efficacy to impose a well-managed communication with the bystanders as well as cultivate their personal and professional identity. This study showed that intense practical training, simulations, and seminars on repeat courses should be held to enhance the student nurses’ experience.

7.IMPACT OF STRESS ON THE ACADEMIC PERFORMANCE OF THE BACHELOR OF SCIENCE IN NURSING STUDENTS OF CAMARINES SUR POLYTECHNIC COLLEGES 
    -Stress is a natural human response that teaches people how to overcome challenges and stay safe. Everyone experiences times of stress.  Their general state of well-being is greatly impacted by how they manage stress. The researchers investigated the impact of stress well-being on the academic performance of the Bachelor of Science in Nursing students of Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges, such as the physical, mental, spiritual, and social aspects of a person, as well as its effect on the student’s academic performance. The respondents are from Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges students enrolled in the Bachelor of Science in Nursing program for the 2022–2023 academic year. There are 1431 students enrolled in the Bachelor of Science in Nursing, Level I through IV combined. A questionnaire served as the main data collection instrument for this study, and the researchers used a descriptive-correlational methodology. As the forms were retrieved, all of the data was tallied and compiled. The tools used were the Weighted Mean, the Percentage Technique, and the Chi-Square test for independence. The majority of the respondents are female, financial support system comes from their parents, and have a family monthly income of Php 10,000 to 24,999, and most of the respondents are 20 years old, first year students, middle children in the family. In terms of intrinsic factors; anxiety related to exams and grades is the major cause of stress for nursing students; issues with time management and workload balancing are the major cause of stress for the extrinsic factors. There is a moderate impact of stress on the academic performance of the students. The profile age, sex, year level, ordinal position, financial support system, and family monthly income showed that there is no significant relationship between knowledge, skills, and attitude. In terms of the relationship between factors that cause stress and the impact of stress, some of the factors are not significantly related. The proposed plan can help mitigate the impact of stress on the academic performance of the students. 

8. IMPACT OF THE INCREASE IN ENROLLMENT IN THE NURSING PROGRAM AT CAMARINES SUR POLYTECHNIC COLLEGES,  NABUA, CAMARINES SUR 
    -Nurses play a vital role at the forefront of our society. As a result, many aspiring nurses have entered nursing schools by accepting the dynamic challenges while pursuing this profession, seeing how the world requires nurses, and what impact could they possibly make on their patients and the healthcare system. This study aimed to determine the impact of the increase in enrollment in the nursing program at Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges, Nabua Camarines Sur, the factors that motivate a student to pursue the nursing program, the significant relationship between identified variables, the difference on the responses of the randomly selected respondents, and propose a plan that could enhance the welfare of the nursing students at Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges. The study utilized a questionnaire and was disseminated through Google Forms among 302 nursing students of Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges composed of randomly selected students from level I to level III. Several statistical tools were employed to accurately quantify the response of the respondents such as percentage technique, Chi Squared Test of Independence, and Kruskal-Wallis test. Results showed that the respondents are from the age group of 19-21 years old, the majority were female and first-year level nursing, and have a family monthly income of below 10,000. The impact of the increase in enrollment in the nursing program in terms of faculty was interpreted as Moderately Affect. In terms of facilities, the interpretation was Moderately Affect. For the intrinsic factors that motivate a student to pursue the nursing program, it was interpreted as Greatly Affect. In terms of extrinsic factors, the interpretation was Greatly Affect. In addition, there is a significant relationship between the age of the respondents and the impact of the increase in enrollment in terms of faculty and facilities. Moreover, there is a significant difference between the responses of the thre eyear levels on the impact of the increase in enrollment in terms of facilities. The welfare of nursing students can be enhanced by creating teaching materials, such as a booklet outlining various nursing theories and skills, and disseminating information to nursing students about the execution of the course syllabus for better understanding. Moreover, the use of a room plotting system and room occupancy monitoring allows the students to have equal opportunity and time to occupy rooms for class, creating a better and more conducive learning environment.

9. HOSPITAL PREFERENCES AMONG THE RESIDENTS OF SAN FRANCISCO, IRIGA CITY IN SEEKING HEALTHCARE SERVICES
    -The study determined the hospital preferences of the residents of San Francisco, Iriga City in seeking health care services.  Also, it establishes the factors that influence the respondent’s hospital preferences. The study used a descriptive correlational method to describe the findings through a self-made checklist questionnaire which was administered to a sample population of 250 respondents who are the heads of the families from zones 4 and 6 of San Francisco Iriga, City. Findings revealed that most of the respondents were 36 years old and above, female. married, with a monthly family income below 11,000.00, and most of them completed a high school education. Hospital preferences of the respondents were based on three aspects such as physical structure, services, and personnel. The respondents preferred private hospital’s physical structure while they prefer the government hospital’ services. However, the respondents preferred both private and government hospital’s personnel respectively. Most of the respondents preferred the physical structure of the private hospitals because of its accessibility, ventilation and lighting, infrastructure, and safety and comfort. Moreover, health programs and services, availability of drugs and supplies as well as its affordability were the characteristics that the respondents preferred the most in government hospital. The result further shows that the residents strongly agreed that several factors have influenced their hospital preferences in seeking healthcare services. Some are as follows: The cost of services, Efficiency and Availability of the Health Care providers, Quality of health care services provided, Healthcare workers' attributes like attitude and the way they render, healthcare services, Health care services offered, Hospital location and environment, The type of health care needs, Importance of continuity of care, Availability of equipment or modern equipment, State-of-the-Art healthcare facility. Furthermore, age, sex, civil status, educational attainment, and family monthly income do not have a significant impact on the hospital preference among the residents of San Francisco, Iriga City.

10. READINESS OF NEW NURSING GRADUATES FROM CAMARINES SUR POLYTECHNIC COLLEGES IN TRANSITIONING TOWARDS BEGINNING NURSE
    -New nursing graduates face a sudden change in environment and responsibilities as they take a role in the nursing profession. Consequently, difficulties and challenges may arise and affect their views of their career. This study investigates the Readiness of the New Nursing Graduates from the Camarines Sur Polytechnic College in transitioning towards beginning nursing. A total of eighty-six (86) newly graduated nursing students of Batch 2022 from the aforementioned school participated in the study by means of a structured questionnaire. Moreover, this study utilizes a descriptive correlational research design to depict relationships among variables under study. Statistical tools such as percentage technique, weighted mean, Spearman’s Rho, and Kendall’s coefficient of concordance were used in analyzing and treating the gathered data. Findings of the study indicates that new graduate nurses (NGNs) are ready and perceive themselves equipped with sufficient knowledge, skills, and attitude to transition toward beginning nurses. Nonetheless, challenges such as fear of embarrassment and failure, environmental stress, and work culture occur throughout their transition phase. The study further revealed a significant correlation between the respondent’s General Weighted Average and challenges the NGNs faced. Results of this study implicates the crucial role of the student and the institutions in the production of professionally well-prepared BSN graduates. Furthermore, this study can aid in providing supervision for future graduates and assist them to enhance their readiness and minimize the possible challenges that they may encounter once they are the one under the process of transition.

11. CHALLENGES EXPERIENCED BY TEENAGE PARENTS IN SELECTED BARANGAYS OF BAAO, CAMARINES SUR 
    -Navigating the complexities of parenthood is a formidable challenge, and when this journey commences during the teenage years, the hurdles intensify. These challenges underscore the importance of thorough preparation and understanding the multifaceted responsibilities involved in nurturing a child amidst the evolving landscape of their own adolescence. This study aimed to determine the challenges experienced by teenage parents in the selected barangays of Baao, Camarines Sur to address and alleviate the difficulties experienced by the respondents specifically on physical, psycho-emotional, psycho-social, financial, and parental role challenges and to identify their coping. A questionnaire with 10 questions or indicators for the challenges experienced and 15 questions regarding the strategies they utilized in coping. The respondents of the study were teenage parents aged 15-19 years old. Out of 57 cases of teenage parenting aged 15 – 19 years old of selected barangays (five from the top ten with most teenage pregnancy cases in the year 2021-2022) in Baao, Camarines Sur namely: La Medalla, San Vicente, Salvacion, San Francisco, and San Roque, twenty (20) participated in the study. The master list was acquired from the Rural Health Unit of Baao. There is a noticeable relationship between the educational attainment of teenage parents and their physical challenges. Financial challenges also show a strong correlation with family monthly income and the occupations of both parents. Moreover, the mother's occupation is significantly linked to parental role challenges. However, none of the profile indicators show a significant relationship with psychoemotional and psychosocial challenges. The relationship of parental role challenges experienced by the respondents and the strategies they utilized to address these challenges are significant. Wherein the several strategies employed by the respondents do not have a significant relationship. A proposed plan of educational booklet was made focused on addressing the challenges that strongly resonated with the respondents.

12. READINESS IN PERFORMING BEGINNING NURSES’ ROLES AMONG LEVEL IV NURSING STUDENTS OF CAMARINES SUR POLYTECHNIC COLLEGES
     -Readiness is essential for passively adjusting to changes, and nurses do this frequently. As for nursing students, this includes case-based learning, research, constructive skills development, and the use of interprofessional learning activities. To satisfy the purpose of this study, the researchers want to determine the Readiness in Performing Beginning Nurses’ Roles among Level IV Nursing Students of Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges. Specifically, involving the profile, level of readiness in performing beginning nurse's role in terms of the national nursing core competency standards in the Philippines, challenges met, and significant relationship between the profile and level of readiness of the respondents. A quantitative research design was utilized by the researchers. Hence, questionnaires were used to generate data from the Level IV nursing students of Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges who were chosen and whose responses provided adequate information to determine the readiness of Level IV nursing students of Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges in performing beginning nurse's role. The findings of this study revealed that the majority of level IV nursing students belonged to the age group of 20-22 years old, most are females, most of them have a P20,000-P30,000 family income, and mostly Roman Catholic. The Level IV nursing students are highly competent under the beginning nurse's roles on client care, moderately competent under the beginning nurse's roles on management and leadership, and moderately competent under the beginning nurse's roles on research. Thus, there are three topmost indicators which the level IV nursing students agreed as the indicator that strongly affects them which are Financial Problem, Lack of Classroom, and Stress. Therefore, it can be implied that there is a significant relationship between the profile of the respondents and the Readiness in Performing Beginning Nurses’ Roles among Level IV Nursing Students of Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges.

13. SKILLS DEVELOPMENT OF LEVEL III NURSING STUDENTS IN NCM 103 OF CAMARINES SUR POLYTECHNIC COLLEGES, NABUA, CAMARINES SUR
     -The pandemic has greatly impacted clinical learning practices in our country. Students, clinical instructors, and employees have been unable to go to school for their safety and to prevent the spread of the virus. Nursing schools have been closed, especially during the peak of the Covid19 pandemic. To address this, the government has implemented online learning as an alternative, but it has its challenges, particularly for subjects like Fundamentals of Nursing Practices, which require hands-on training. To satisfy the purpose of this study, the researchers conducted a study to classify the skills development of nursing students regarding the new normal of fundamental nursing practices and how this affects the learning of a nursing student in skill laboratories, specifically the effect of student, instructor and environmental factors. A quantitative descriptive research was utilized the skills development in NCM 103 and the actions taken by nursing students.  Questionnaires were used to collect data from randomly selected third-year nursing students at Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges, whose responses provided adequate information to determine the skills development in NCM 103. The findings of this study revealed that the majority of level III nursing students agree that various factors, including student, instructor, and environmental factors, have significantly influenced their skills development in NCM 103. The data showed that the actions taken by level III nursing students to cope with learning moderately affect their skills development in NCM 103. Therefore, it can be implied that there is a significant relationship between the demographic profile of level III nursing students and the factors that affect their skills development in NCM 103. As a result, there are recommendations to effectively enhance the skills development of NCM 103 for CSPC nursing students
